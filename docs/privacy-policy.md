# Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-17_

This Privacy Policy describes how this Discord modmail bot ("the Bot", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and stores information when you interact with it on Discord. By using the Bot — either by sending it a direct message or by participating in a Discord server where it is installed — you agree to this policy.

> **Note:** This document is a boilerplate intended as a starting point. Each operator of the Bot is independently responsible for reviewing and adapting it to their own jurisdiction, server, and use case. It is not legal advice.

## 1. Who Operates the Bot

This Bot is self-hosted software based on the open-source [Modmail Bot](https://github.com/Dragory/modmailbot) project. The instance you are interacting with is operated by the server staff who installed and configured it ("the Operator"). The Operator — not the project maintainers — is the controller of any personal data processed by their instance.

If you have questions about how your data is handled, please contact the Operator of the server where you encountered the Bot.

## 2. Information We Collect

The Bot processes the following information in order to function:

### 2.1 Information you provide directly

- **Direct messages** you send to the Bot, including text content, attachments, embeds, stickers, and any other media.
- **Replies and messages** sent by server staff through the Bot.

### 2.2 Information collected automatically from Discord

- **Discord user ID, username, and display name** of users who message the Bot or interact with modmail threads.
- **Discord server (guild) ID and channel IDs** related to modmail threads.
- **Message IDs and timestamps** for messages relayed through the Bot.
- **Role information** of staff members who interact with threads (used to determine permissions).

### 2.3 Information generated by the Bot

- **Modmail thread metadata**: who opened the thread, when it was opened and closed, who replied, internal staff notes, snippets used, and edits or deletions.
- **Logs** of thread activity, which may be exported as text or HTML log files.
- **Configuration data** set by staff (snippets, allowed roles, blocked users, etc.).

The Bot does **not** intentionally collect:
- Information from servers where it is not installed.
- Voice data.
- Payment or financial information.
- Information about users who have not interacted with the Bot.

## 3. How Information Is Used

Collected information is used solely to:

- Relay messages between users and server staff.
- Maintain a record of past conversations so staff can reference them for context.
- Enforce moderation actions such as blocking abusive users.
- Operate, debug, and improve the Bot instance.

We do not sell, rent, or share your information with third parties for marketing purposes.

## 4. How Information Is Stored

- All data is stored locally by the Operator, typically in a SQLite database (`db/data.sqlite`) and in log files on the server hosting the Bot.
- Attachments sent to the Bot may be saved locally to a folder on the host (`attachments/`) for later retrieval by staff.
- Storage duration depends entirely on the Operator's retention practices. By default, the Bot does not automatically delete old threads or logs.
- Data is not transmitted to the project maintainers of the open-source Modmail Bot project.

## 5. Who Can See Your Information

- **Server staff** with appropriate roles in the Discord server can view modmail threads, including the full contents of your messages to the Bot.
- **The Operator** and anyone with administrative access to the host running the Bot can access the underlying database, attachments, and logs.
- **Discord, Inc.** processes the messages in transit as part of operating the Discord platform; their handling is governed by [Discord's Privacy Policy](https://discord.com/privacy).

## 6. Third-Party Services

The Bot relies on the [Discord API](https://discord.com/developers/docs) to function. Your use of Discord itself is governed by Discord's own terms and privacy policy. Individual Operators may also configure plugins or integrations that send data to additional third parties; if so, the Operator is responsible for disclosing those.

## 7. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction (for example, under the GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, or PIPEDA), you may have rights to:

- Request access to the personal data the Bot holds about you.
- Request correction or deletion of that data.
- Object to or restrict processing.
- Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

To exercise any of these rights, contact the Operator of the server you interacted with. Note that deletion of message logs may also remove records useful to staff for moderation context.

## 8. Children

The Bot is not directed at children under the minimum age required to use Discord in their country (generally 13). If you believe a child has interacted with the Bot, contact the Operator so the relevant data can be removed.

## 9. Security

The Operator is responsible for securing the machine on which the Bot runs. While reasonable measures should be taken to protect stored data, no system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security of information stored or transmitted by the Bot.

## 10. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top will reflect the latest revision. Continued use of the Bot after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

## 11. Contact

For questions, requests, or complaints regarding this Privacy Policy, contact the Operator of the Discord server where the Bot is in use.
